Get ready to apply

If you have not applied yet

The last day to apply is March 31, 2023.

If you’re eligible and you recently filed your 2021 taxes, you may not be able to apply on time through CRA My Account or by phone. In this case, apply with the online application form by March 31, 2023.

If you meet the eligibility criteria, complete any steps you are missing so you're ready to apply.

File your income taxes

Filing your income tax return is required to apply for this benefit.

For information on how to file, go to: Get ready to do your taxes.

You can apply for either the Canada Child Benefit (CCB) or GST/HST credit to provide your personal information and your world income.

If you had children under 18 years old in 2021

Submit a statement of income as part of a Canada Child Benefit (CCB) application for 2021.

By applying for the CCB, you will also automatically apply for the GST/HST credit.

If you did not have children under 18 years old in 2021

Submit a statement of income as part of a GST/HST Credit and climate action incentive payment application for 2021.

If you have a spouse or common-law partner, they will also need to file their 2021 taxes or submit a statement of income.

If you already applied and only the 2021 world income is missing, you can call the benefit enquiries service at 1-800-387-1193 (opens up phone application)1-800-387-1193 to provide your world income.

You will need to file your 2021 taxes before you can apply.

Generally, your return will be assessed within 2 weeks for electronically filed returns or 8 weeks for mailed returns.

If you have a spouse or common-law partner, they will also need to file their 2021 taxes or submit a statement of income.

If you have no taxable income to report in 2021, you still need to report your income as zero on your 2021 tax return to qualify for the benefit.

For information on how to file, go to: Get ready to do your taxes (opens in new window).

Since you filed your 2021 tax return, you're ready for the next step.

  1. You can recover your user ID or password by going to CRA My Account (opens in new window)
    • Select 'CRA sign in'
    • Choose 'Forgot your user ID' or 'Forgot your password'
      • If you 'Forgot your user ID' you will need your:
        • social insurance number (SIN)
        • date of birth
        • copy of this year's or last year's assessed individual tax return

Direct deposit will be the fastest way to get your payment.

Your mailing address must be up to date:

  • Online: Confirm it's up to date in your CRA My Account (opens in new window) under your personal profile
  • By phone: Call  1-800-959-8281 1-800-959-8281

Once your application is processed, a cheque will take about 10 business days. If you want your payment faster, direct deposit payment will take up to 5 business days.
