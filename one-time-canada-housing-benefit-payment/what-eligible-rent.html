What is eligible rent

Rent eligible for the One-time top-up to the Canada Housing Benefit refers to a payment you made in 2022 under a lease, licence or similar arrangement to occupy a principal residence in 2022.

On this page

  • Amounts that are not considered as rent
  • Meals, board or other services are not included
  • Rent payments shared by multiple individuals (roommates) or families are claimed separately
  • Marital status changes may affect how rent is claimed
  • More than one principal residence and how to claim rent
    • If you lived apart from your spouse or common-law partner

Amounts that are not considered as rent

For this benefit, do not include rent amounts:

  • paid to a relative if they did not report them as rental income on their tax return
  • paid for utilities or property taxes that were not part of the rental arrangement
  • paid for utilities or property taxes to a person or entity who was not a party to the lease, licence or arrangement
  • specified on the lease for meals, board or other services (Read about unspecified amount of rent for meals, board or other services)
  • paid that will be deducted as a business expense
  • paid for rent to own or other capital lease type arrangement, where all or a portion of the rent goes towards the purchase of the property

Meals, board or other services are not included

Do not include any payment that is specified on the lease for meals, board or other services.

If there is an amount of rent that is not specified on the lease and that goes towards meals, board, or other services, include only 90% of the rent.

Rent payments shared by multiple individuals (roommates) or families are claimed separately

In the case of rent payments shared by multiple individuals (roommates) or families living at the same address, you would:

  • each apply as separate family units or individuals
  • each claim the portion of the rent that you paid

Marital status changes may affect how rent is claimed

If you got married in 2022

Before or on December 1, 2022:

If you got married in 2022 before or on December 1, 2022, only one spouse is eligible to receive the one-time payment. If you are the spouse who applies:

  • include rent you paid in 2022 before you were married
  • include rent your spouse paid in 2022 before you were married
  • include rent you paid as a married couple in 2022

After December 1, 2022:

If you got married in 2022 after December 1, 2022, your marital status remains, for the purpose of this benefit, as it was prior to you getting married. This means, if you were single or widowed and you got married after December 1, 2022, then you would still be considered as single or widowed for this benefit.

In your application:

  • include the total amount of rent you paid in 2022 up to the day before you got married
  • include your portion of the total amount of rent paid in 2022 by you and your cohabiting spouse or common-law partner from the day you got married
If you got separated or divorced in 2022

Separation is when a married or common-law couple lives apart more than 90 days, due to a breakdown in their relationship. To be considered separated for this benefit, you must have been separated for 90 days or more by December 1, 2022.

Before or on December 1, 2022:

If you got separated or divorced in 2022 before or on December 1, 2022, each spouse or common-law partner is eligible to receive the one-time payment.

In your application:

  • include your portion of the total amount of rent paid in 2022 by you and your cohabiting spouse or common-law partner up to the day before you started living separate and apart
  • include the total amount of rent you paid in 2022 starting on the first day you were living separate and apart

In your former spouse’s or common-law partner’s application, they would:

  • include their portion of the total amount of rent paid in 2022 by you and your cohabiting spouse or common-law partner up to the day before you started living separate and apart
  • include the total amount of rent they paid in 2022 starting on the first day you were living separate and apart

After December 1, 2022:

If you got separated or divorced in 2022 after December 1, 2022, your marital status remains, for the purpose of this benefit, as it was prior to you getting separated or divorced. This means you would still be considered married for this benefit.

Only one spouse or common-law partner is eligible to receive the one-time payment. In your application:

  • include rent you paid as a married couple in 2022
  • include rent you paid in 2022 after you were separated or divorced
  • include rent your former spouse paid in 2022 after you were separated or divorced
If you became widowed in 2022

If you became widowed in 2022, do the following in your application:

  • include the total amount of rent paid in 2022 by you and your cohabiting spouse or common-law partner up to the day before you became widowed
  • include the total amount of rent you paid in 2022 starting on the first day you became widowed

More than one principal residence and how to claim rent

If you lived apart from your spouse or common-law partner

You and your spouse or common-law partner might live in two principal residences due to physical separation (for example, one spouse goes away to school or one common-law partner is away for work purposes).

If you and your spouse or common-law partner lived in two different principal residences at any point in 2022, and living apart was not due to a breakdown in the relationship, only one spouse or common-law partner is eligible to receive the one-time payment. If you are the spouse or common-law partner who applies:

  • include the total amount of rent paid in 2022 for both principal residences, regardless of which spouse or common-law partner paid the rent

Living apart due to a breakdown in the relationship

If, due to a breakdown in the relationship, you and your spouse or common-law partner lived apart at least 90 days, you are considered separated by the CRA. Determine your rent if your marital status changed in 2022.

If you moved

If you moved during 2022, include eligible rent paid in 2022 for all principal residences you lived in during 2022 for the time that they were your primary residence.
