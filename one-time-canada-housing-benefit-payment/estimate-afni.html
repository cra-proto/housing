Estimate your income and rent

Estimate your average family net income for the housing benefit to check if:

  • it is $35,000 or less for families, or $20,000 or less for individuals

If you have access a CRA My Account, you can alternatively look up your average family net income for the benefit by going to the application form.

Did you have one or more dependants (a child, minor or disabled adult) on December 1, 2022? (required)

Who counts as a dependant?

Who counts as a dependant?

A dependant is someone who lives with you and relies on you for financial support. This includes:

  • your parent or grandparent
  • your child, grandchild, or sibling under 18 years of age
  • your child, grandchild, or sibling 18 years of age or older with an impairment in physical or mental functions

If your dependant usually lives with you when not in school, the CRA considers that dependant to live with you.

Universal child care benefit

The UCCB was a taxable benefit paid for children under 18 years of age, available to all eligible individuals, regardless of their income. Payments ended as of July 2016, but lump-sum amounts continue to be paid for months prior to that date. The Canada Child Benefit (CCB), which is an income-tested, non-taxable benefit, replaced the UCCB in 2016.

Refer to Line 11700 – Universal child care benefit (UCCB) for more information
