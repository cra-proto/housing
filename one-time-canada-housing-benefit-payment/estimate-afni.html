What is adjusted net income

Adjusted net income is your household net income, minus any universal child care benefit (UCCB) and registered disability savings plan (RDSP) income received, plus any UCCB and RDSP amounts repaid.

Your household net income includes your net income and your spouse's net income from all sources inside and outside Canada.

What is living common-law?

This applies to a person who is not your spouse, with whom you are living in a conjugal relationship.

Did you have one or more dependants (a child, minor or disabled adult) on December 1, 2022? (required)

Who counts as a dependant?

Who counts as a dependant?

You are considered to have a dependant for this application if:

  • you live with and financially support your child, grandchild, or sibling under 18 years of age on December 1, 2022
    • add or update children under your care through the Canada Child Benefit (CCB)
  • you claimed a deduction under line 30400 of your 2021 income tax return for any of the following:
    • your parent or grandparent
    • your child, grandchild, or sibling 18 years of age or older with impairment in physical or mental functions

Universal child care benefit

The UCCB was a taxable benefit paid for children under 18 years of age, available to all eligible individuals, regardless of their income. Payments ended as of July 2016, but lump-sum amounts continue to be paid for months prior to that date. The Canada Child Benefit (CCB), which is an income-tested, non-taxable benefit, replaced the UCCB in 2016.

Refer to Line 11700 – Universal child care benefit (UCCB) for more information
