You will need to apply for either the Canada Child Benefit (CCB) or the GST/HST Credit to submit a statement of income for 2021.

If you had children under 18 years old in 2021

Submit a statement of income (RC66SCH) as part of your CCB application.

By applying for the CCB, you will also automatically apply for the GST/HST credit.

We will need your 2021 income information before we can determine if you are eligible for the One-time top-up to the Canada Housing Benefit.

If you did not have children under 18 years old in 2021

Submit a statement of income (RC151) as part of a GST/HST Credit application.

We will need your 2021 income information before we can determine if you are eligible for the One-time top-up to the Canada Housing Benefit.

If you have a spouse or common-law partner, they will also need to file their 2021 taxes or submit a statement of income.

You will need to file your 2021 taxes before you can apply.

Generally, your return will be assessed within 2 weeks for electronically filed returns or 8 weeks for mailed returns. You should ensure your return is assessed before you apply for the one-time payment.

If you have no taxable income to report in 2021, you still need to report no income in a tax return for 2021 to qualify for the benefit.
